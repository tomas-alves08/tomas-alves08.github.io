Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

People understanding they are able to master any skill fires them with hope and hope is the most power weapon an individual could have. They then know that they would be able to level up their skills in literally anything they would truly commit to.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

Believing you can learn anything gives you the feeling that nothing is impossible and the sky is the limit. Because I have seen so many people saying how lucky I am for having some of the talents I have but they don't how wrong they are because most of the talents I have were not given to me but acquired with a huge effort and more than that the perseverence that I, one day, would be able to achieve something I really wanted to achieve.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

To be very honest, I often think if I was able to learn how to play music and also able to speak english coming from a non english speaking country, I am able to learn programming. Live a life speaking a different language other than your own is so overwhelming at times. It requires an increadible effort. So, if I face that day after day, it won't be any programming language that will stop me. So, the approach I use to increase neuroplasticity is trying day after day, and not giving up, believing one day all the things that I struggle today with will be simple and I will be laughing in the future on how difficult I used to find it. Positivity is the best way for a happy life.

Link to a resource that you found particularly useful or engaging.

Reading the 7 habits of highly effective people by Sthephen Covey helped me on how to behave in order to be best version of myself I could ever become. Trying to incorporate those steps, specially the ones that were not natural for me helps me to define how I will face some challenges I have in real life. Sometimes it's just easier if you just become a reactive person other than a pro-active person. However, I try not to give up at times because I don't want to be average on anything I do. Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

Your mind defines who you are. So, no matter you don't have certain skill if you have the positive approach that you can learn and you can be great on it. Your mindset is more important than any skill. Beucase I person who has got a great skill but no positive mindset, has got only a great skill. However, someone who has got no skill but a great positive mindset can have any skill and can master it obviously with resilience and hard work.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I did know we all could learn things and I had heard before of the brain creating thicker pathways if you practice something over and over again. However I did not know the brain was phisically changing as well, growing in certain areas. But yes, it makes total sense. If the brain pathway for certain skill is becoming thicker is natural to think that more neurons are getting there. However, when I was at school I learned that neurons were cells that were not able to duplicate, so it was counter intuitive for me. Very interesting anyway.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I will repeat to myself everyday that one day I will be able to become a great programmer and that's the reason that I am practicing it so much. It will keep my focus and will remind me why I am studying so hard on those days I want to give up.

Link to a resource that you found particularly useful or engaging.

The video of Kobe Bryant saying how a winner must think was particular engaging for me. He makes it very clear it ain't easy and that it's actually a choice someone makes. But are you ready to pay for that? Are you ready to pay for success? Success will not be given to you, you got to go out there and bring it home. You got to own it, you got to deserve it.
